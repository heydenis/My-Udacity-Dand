Project: Investigate the TMDB movie database¶

Introduction¶

I selected the TMDb database for this analysis: the dataset contains information about 10.000 movies.

This analysis will look at movie directors and their relationship with revenues. We will try to answers questions like:

  • Which movie directors earned the most?
  • Do movie directors have a track record? How many movies did they produced?
  • Are they efficient with their budget?

No machine learning and/or descriptive statistics have been used, findings are then tentative.

Notes about the dataset:¶

Certain columns, like ‘cast’ and ‘genres’, contain multiple values separated by pipe (|) characters. There are some odd characters in the ‘cast’ column. Don’t worry about cleaning them. You can leave them as is. The final two columns ending with “_adj” show the budget and revenue of the associated movie in terms of 2010 dollars, accounting for inflation over time.

More info at: https://www.kaggle.com/tmdb/tmdb-movie-metadata

Conclusions¶

Defining a "good" director can be subjective, we see how directors that on average scored better ratings are not the ones with the highest movie revenues. We also see that, on a dataset of movies grossing more than average, the distribution of ratings is distributed between 4 and 8: even bad movies can have good revenues.

But, talking about reviews or talking about box office, there is one common point: constance is required.
